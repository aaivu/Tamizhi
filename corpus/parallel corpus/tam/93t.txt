           PRESDENT'S MESSAGE

Lady Lochore Loan Fund was established on 26 March 1927 on a
financial donation of £300 from a Scottish national, Lady Jean Lochore. As of today
for eight decades the Fund has shouldered an excellent role of minimizing the
burden of debt faced by employees of public and private sector who have been
burrowing from the private sector which is the reason that the Fund was established
and has engaged on a successful journey forward.

Especially, Today the community has faced a major transformation in the
economic, political, religious, ethnic and educational sphere than in 1927.
Accordingly, long term, medium term and short term debt schemes have been
introduced to meet the diverse loan requirements of public and private sector
employees while providing successful solutions for these transformative courses. In
consideration of the types of loans in the existing Commercial banking system, these
loan schemes introduced are more conducive and flexible in terms of credit
conditions, collateral requirements, loan interest rates and recovery period. As a
result, the number of applicants seeking for loans has increased steadily.

During my tenure of office for the last three years, a special programme
had been launched to transform the functions of the Fund beyond the traditional role
of the Lady Lochore Loan Fund.

Especially, the matters such as operating activities of the Fund in
accordance with existing rules and regulations, utilizing the latest technology in the
operation of functions of the Fund whenever possible, designing the operations of
the organization to suit future economic developments, providing necessary
facilities and incentives for the employees to accomplish more friendly and efficient
service, introducing and maintaining an appropriate Accounting system to suit with
more transparent accountability method of resource utilization of the Fund, and
applying Public Sector Accounting Standards for the preparation of accounts to
subject the functions of the Fund to the Auditor General's direct audit are included
with the same.

In comparison with the previous year, Significant progress had been made in
financial performance of the Fund. The surplus of pre-tax profit has increased by
11% over the previous year and the Fund had to pay 26.7% of heavy tax in the year
2018 compared to the year 2017 due to non-receipt of tax relief as in previous
years. AS a result, the surplus after the tax deduction is reduced by 7%.

Considering the financial position in the year 2018, the financial stability of
the Fund has increased and especially the net asset value has approximately
increased by 9% especially compared to the year 2017. Accordingly, the stability of
the Fund has been steadily improved in every year and especially, it is a great
achievement that the Fund has been able to operate within and build such a strong
financial position without receiving any funds from the Ministry of Finance, and it
would be a great example for other Institutions that rely on Government Funds.


Also, the contributions of all the employees including the Board of Trustees
to the progress of the Fund should be commended. It is very difficult for the
financial institutions engaged with such matters to show a continuous progress
especially on annual basis. The financial losses incurred by the Fund especially due
to default in debt repayment are immense. However, due to the recovery
methodology followed by the Fund, such loan repayment defaults could be kept to
a minimum level.

The Fund has developed and employee incentive scheme to enhance
employee motivation based on their performance, and it is an additional
qualification for the efficient employees to receive higher incentive on their
commitment and on their promotions.

Accordingly, 1 assure that 1 will act with the full cooperation of the staff to
further develop its performance shown during the year 2018 with the objective of
making the Fund as the foremost public sector lending institution in the Future.

M ESSAGE OF MANAGING TRUSTEE

Lady Lochore Loan Fund (LLLF) was
founded on February 17“ February, 1927
by Lady Jean Lochore, who later became
a Citizen of Sri Lanka (Ceylon), and wife
of a Millionaire Businessman from
Scotland, UK, with the help of Rev. Fr.
CE.V. Nathanielz of Colombo and
established under the Lady Lochore Loan
Fund Act No.38 of 1951 under the
Ministry of Finance. Since then it has
been managed by the Managing Trustee
under the supervision and direction of a
Board of Trustees appointed by the
Minister of Finance. took over office of
the LLLF as the Managing Trustee (Head of the Organization) on 27 February, 2015
with the formation of the Yahapalana Government on the 08] anuary, 2015 under HE
President, Maithripala Sirisena and Hon. Prime Minister, Ranil Wickremesinghe.
Being the retired senior Public Servant of the Sri Lanka Administrative Service (SLAS)
and having held high executive positions in the Public Service, my first move was to
study the organization and its operations well and have been able to set my foot on re-
structuring the entity to what it is today with the express approval of the Board of
Trustees. Within two years of my assuming duties as the Managing Trustee the
processes of granting loans to public servants had been Simplified and the
administration, accounting and IT sectors of the LLLF streamlined to meet the
challenges and the needs of the day in the competitive markets faced by the lending
institutions where the socio-economic needs and advanced technology have been rapidly
growing.

The maximum amount of loan of Rs. 50,000/-provided for nearly 30 years has been
raised to higher amounts and today the Public Servants who are in financial distress Can
enjoy our loan scheme that grants Rs. 100,000/- to non-staff Public Servants, Rs.
175,000/- to non-staff Public Servants who stagnate in their initial grades for longer
period of time due to Various reasons, and Rs.250,000/-and Rs.300,000/-to staff-grade
officers in the Public Service.

According to the simplified mechanism in place as at date, the loan application is
processed and released within one week as against nearly two to three months to get the
loan passed in the past. A loan applicant has to visit the LLLF only two occasions as
against five times in the past and the loan application can be downloaded from the LLLF
Website in all three languages from any part of the island nation. We are able to carry
out this enormous task due to the efficiently functioning Board of Trustees and the
magnificent team work of the staff under my able guidance and leadership without any
grant from the Ministry or budgetary allocation from the Government.

However, the LLLF has now begun to face problems in issuing loans within three days
due to lack of funds resulting from big amounts being disbursed as loans and the
demand for loans has increased in multiple proportions. To overcome this crisis, we
have now Started regulating the issue of loans in such a manner that no loan applicant is
refused of granting loans but the time period involved in processing the loan


applications and issuance of cheques is limited to one month duration. It is crystal clear
that the LLLF needs financial assistance from the Ministry to continue to provide a
better service to the majority of the Public Servants and if the Ministry accommodates
the request made by the Board of Trustees to grant the LLLF at least Rs.500Mn as an
interest-free loan to establish ten (10) Branch Offices of the LLLF in 10 selected
District Secretariats to extend our services to those public servants who could reach
those ten Branch Offices with no hassle and inconvenience and obtain loans. The Hon.
Mangala Samaraweera, Minister of Finance having studied our request and understood
the plight of the Public Servants, who appreciate and admire the services rendered by the
LLLF and the importance of its existence, agreed to grant us a loan of Rs.500Mn
through the Budget Proposal of 2020. The 1.4Mn Public Servants of this country will
no doubt be grateful to him and the Government of Sri Lanka.

On the whole, the economic efficiency of the Organization and the productivity level of
the work force have been remarkably enhanced through the staff training scheme put in
place according to its training needs, and the welfare of the work force is given pride of
place to enable them to achieve self-satisfaction and self-actualization in the work place.
A Vibrant Performance Appraisal Scheme has been introduced to decide on the payment
of Annual Bonus to the staff of the LLLF and this scheme has enormously induced the
trend of motivation and empowerment among the staff as a whole.

The building of LLLF which was not repaired for the last 50 years and was in a
dilapidated condition has been fully refurbished with all modern facilities and also
action has already been taken to beautify the office and the reception areas to enable the
workforce and the clients who patronize the LLLF to do their business transactions in a
Very conducive, pleasant and healthy atmosphere.

The number of loans granted was around 3700 in the year 2018,as against around 4100
in the year 2017, and the quantum of loan released per month was around Rs.30Mn the
year 2018 as against Rs.31Mn in the year 2017.The decline in both the issuance of
loans and quantum of loans granted in the year 2018 was due to the decline in the
demand for loans. However, a new loan scheme was planned to introduce in 2019
in order to overcome this situation. Also, the monthly rental received from five tenants
who occupy the 2" and 1“ Floors of LLLF is around Rs.2 Mn, with which the LLLF
meets 3/4 of its monthly recurrent and operational expenditure and the profits
generated from granting of loans are added to the profit of the LLLF. The annual profit
earned by the LLLF after taxation stands at around 41Mn in the year 2017 as against
around Rs.38Mn the year 2018. In fact, we made a strong request to the Hon.
Minister of Finance to direct the Inland Revenue Department to exempt taxing the
income derived from the interest on loans granted to Public Servants so that we
would be able to utilize the exempted amount from taxation to grant more loans to
the deserving public servants. We do agree that the taxation is part and parcel of the
Government revenue earning process but a service oriented Organization lke LLLF
that serves only the public servants who are in financial distress without any grant or
budgetary allocation from the Government, solely depending on its income on the
interest on loans issued, should be exempted from Taxation. The LLLF was
exempted from Income Tax but with the introduction of the new Income Tax Act of
Inland Revenue Act No 24 of 2017 dated of the Inland Revenue Department, this
plivilege was done away with. However, we have made a strong appeal to the Ministry
of Finance to consider exempting the LLLF from income tax through the next Budget.


A Complaint Box is placed for the benefit of the clients who have any
complaint/suggestion to be brought to the notice of the Management and all such
complaints/suggestions are attentively looked into daily and attended to by the
Managing Trustee along with the relevant officials concerned. We found that most of
the complaints made by the customers are focused on undue delays in refunding the
monies in the form of additional installment payments that have come into LLLF
Account. We have been able to rectify this situation by crediting the excess amounts of
money received in the Account of the LLLF, direct to the client's Account enabling
them not to Visit the LLLF for the refund.

The objectives of the LLLF for which it was founded by Lady Jean Lochore have now
been achieved, and all Public Servants in the 25 Districts of Sri Lanka have been able
to enjoy equally and with no discrimination or delay, the loan facilities provided by the
LLLF.

1 would like to make use of this opportunity to extend my sincere gratitude to the Hon.
Minister, State Minister and Secretary of the Ministry of Finance and others who gave
us the guidance and support, and also to the Board of Trustees, officials of the Fund who
stood by me always and made their contributions to make my endeavors successful in
managing the LLLF. 1 would continue to go forward with my creative, innovative and
competitive ideas and efforts to strengthen the LLLF for the betterment of the Public
Servants who have developed great hopes and reliance on LLLF and its yeomen services

BACKGROUND OF THE FUND

SE _9

An initial capital of 300 Sterling pounds for the establishment of the Loan Fund
had been donated by Lady Jean Lochore, who was a Scott national and lived in Sri Lanka
for a long period. Then the Commissioner of Charity, Reverend Father C.E.V.Nathanielz
had taken initiatives to establish the Fund on 26® March 1927 and it was named as Lady
Lochore Loan Fund. The Fund was transformed into a Statutory Body by the Lady
Lochore Loan Fund Act No. 38 of 1951.

Since then by granting loans and
recovering loans, the Fund has functioning as a
revolving fund, as the existing banking System
was not supportive of fulfilling short-term
financial requirements of the employees of the
public and Private Sector Institutions, they were
compelled to obtain loans at very high interest
rates from outside lending organizations or from
individuals. Due to this reason, the employees
of the public sector as well as private sector
were suffering from indebtedness immensely.
The establishment of the Lady Lochore Loan
Fund had brought a great relief to all these
employees concerned. After the establishment
of this Fund as a Statutory Body in 1951, a three
storied building owned by this institution was
constructed with the donations received from
the government and other donors.

2.2 Loan Procedure and the Progress 2018

Employees in Government or Semi government Sector, who have the following
qualifications, could apply for loans from this Fund.

>

Be a pensionable public officer who has completed minimum of 03 years
permanent service in government sector.

A semi-government employee should complete minimum of 05 years of
permanent service.

Should have sufficient service period for recovering the loan amount prior to
the retirement. (The age is considered as 55 years)

Two guarantors for the applicant with qualifications maintained above.

For an employee of a semi government sector, one of the two guarantors
should be a public officer who has completed minimum of 03 years of
permanent service in government sector.

Ability to recover the loaned amount within the 40% limit of loan applicants
monthly salary.

and expenditure as reported in the financial statements and computed in

02. ASSETS AND BASES OF THEIR VALUATION

2.1 Provision for Loan Losses
Provision for Bad and Doubtful Loans are made on the basis of a review
made by the Trustee s loans from customers are Stated at the outstanding

carried forward balances less provision made as at the end of the Reporting
period.

2.2 Fixed deposits

Fixed deposits and other interest bearing securities held for resale in the
near future to benefit from short term market movement are accounted for
at cost plus the relevant proportion of the discount or premium.

2.3 Property, Plant &Equipment

Property Plant & Equipment is stated at cost of purchase together with any
incidental expenses thereon. The assets are stated at cost less accumulated
depreciation, which is provided for on the basis specified below.

Building                                                   4%
Computer Equipment                                  25%
Furniture and Fittings                                10%
Office Equipment                                      10%
Electrical Fixtures and Fittings                    10%
Motor Vehicle                                              25%

From the year 2008/09, the Loan Fund has changed its accounting policy to
provide depreciation from the month of purchase to the month of disposal.
Up to the year 2007/08, depreciation was not provided on the assets

purchased and used during the year also no depreciation was provided in
the year of disposal.

2.4 Other Receivables

Other receivables are stated at the amounts they are estimated to realize.

2.5 Cash & Cash Equivalents

Cash and Cash Equivalents are defined as cash in hand and demand deposit
and short term liquid investment readily convertible to identified amounts
of cash and subject to insignificant risks of changes in Value. The cash
Flow Statement has been prepared using the indirect Method.

03. LIABILITIES AND PROVISIONS

All known liabilities have been provided in preparing the financial
Statements.

3.1 Capital Commitments

Capital commitments of the Loan Fund are disclosed in the respective notes
to the Financial Statements.

3.2 Pensions and Retirement Benefits

A provision has been made for Retiring Gratuity Payable under the
payment of Gratuity Act No.12 of 1983.This Provision has been computed
in accordance with the gratuity formula adopted by the board of Trustees.

3.3 Defined Contribution Plans-Employees Provident Fund and Employees
‘Trust Fund

All employee are eligible for Lady Lochore staff provident Fund
contributions and Employee's Trust Fund contributions in line with
respective Statues and regulations. The 1oan Fund contributes 16% of gross
emoluments or employees to Lady Lochore staff provident Fund and 3%
of gross emoluments of employees to the Employees “Trust Fund which
are externally funded.

04. INCOME STATEMENT

4.1 Revenue Recognition

4.1.1 Interest Income

Interest Income recognized on an accrual basis. The Fund considers Loans
granted to Government employees from whom recoveries have ceased for
3 months as ‘Non Performing Loans’. Thereafter the interest is recognized
on such loans on Cash basis.

4.1.2 Rental Income

Rent income is recognized on an accrual basis.

4.1.3 Expenditure Recognition

All expenditure incurred in the running of the Loan Fund and in
maintaining the capital assets in a State of efficiency have been charge to
revenue in arriving at the profit for the year.

All expenditure incurred in the acquisition, extension or improvement of
assets of a permanent nature in order to carry on or increase the earning
capacity of the Loan Fund has been treated as capital expenditure.
